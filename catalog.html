Chicken Curry

Chicken Curry (Kukul Mas Curry) is a classic Sri Lankan dish featuring tender chicken pieces cooked in a flavorful blend of spices and coconut milk. The curry is infused with the aromatic combination of cinnamon, cardamom, and cloves, creating a rich and satisfying taste. The addition of tomatoes and a medley of spices enhances the depth of flavor, resulting in a hearty and delicious curry. It's commonly served with rice or roti.

Dhal Curry

Dhal Curry (Parippu Curry) is a comforting and nutritious lentil dish that is a staple in Sri Lankan cuisine. Red lentils are cooked to perfection and infused with a tempering of mustard seeds, cumin, and curry leaves. The addition of turmeric and chili powder provides a warm and mildly spicy flavor. This wholesome dish is often served with rice and complements other Sri Lankan curries beautifully.

Coconut Sambal

Coconut Sambol (Pol Sambola) is a vibrant and zesty condiment that adds a burst of flavor to any Sri Lankan meal. Freshly grated coconut is combined with finely chopped red onion, green chilies, and a touch of lime juice. The result is a refreshing and slightly spicy sambol that can be enjoyed as a side dish with rice or flatbread. Its simplicity allows the natural sweetness of coconut to shine through.

Egg Hoppers

Egg Hoppers (Appa) are a popular Sri Lankan breakfast or snack characterized by thin, bowl-shaped pancakes with a perfectly cooked egg nestled in the center. The batter, made from a combination of rice flour and coconut milk, ferments to achieve a unique texture and flavor. Hoppers are typically served with a side of spicy sambol or accompanied by savory curries, offering a delightful culinary experience.
